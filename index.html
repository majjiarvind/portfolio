Case studies

A closer look at how three specific pieces of work actually happened — not just the outcome.

Backlog · CM

Ericsson ENM — Configuration Management

Challenge

Roadmap priorities from Ericsson Product Management needed to become sprint-ready backlog items, but requirements arrived at varying levels of detail from multiple Study Analysts.

Role

Owned the CM backlog end-to-end — worked with Study Analysts to clarify requirements, wrote user stories and acceptance criteria, and refined priority against engineering capacity and dependencies.

Outcome

Sprint predictability improved from 70% to 90% through tighter Definition of Ready and dependency tracking.

Backlog · FM/PM

Ericsson ENM — Fault & Performance Management

Challenge

Production defects were leaking into later stages of delivery, often traced back to unclear or incomplete acceptance criteria at the story level.

Role

Strengthened acceptance criteria quality, supported earlier QA validation, and ran feature-impact analysis on new requests before they entered the backlog.

Outcome

Production defect leakage reduced by 30%, with fewer issues surfacing after release.

AI Tooling

AI-Powered Dashboard for Product Owners

Challenge

Sprint progress, engineer workload, and delivery health were scattered across Jira, Confluence, and Jenkins — no single view existed for Product Owners or Project Managers.

Role

Configured and integrated the KIRO AI agent with Jira, Confluence, and Jenkins via the Model Context Protocol, then designed role-based dashboards on top of it for POs, PMs, Scrum Masters, and engineers.

Outcome

Product Owner documentation productivity improved by 50%, with real-time visibility replacing manual status chasing.

About

I own the product & backlog for Ericsson Network Manager (ENM) across Configuration, Fault, and Performance Management, translating roadmap priorities and business requirements into user stories, acceptance criteria, and sprint-ready backlog items.

I configured and integrated an enterprise AI agent (KIRO) with our Jira, Confluence, and Jenkins environment using the Model Context Protocol, then built role-based dashboards on top of it so Product Owners, Project Managers, and engineers can all see delivery health in real time.

I work daily with TCS Project Managers, Study Analysts, developers, QA, and Ericsson stakeholders, running sprint ceremonies, leading demos, and turning feedback into the next sprint's priorities.
